The semi- batch reactor is often referred to as unsteady state flow reactor and much more difficult to analyze than the batch reactor. It offers good control of reaction speed because reaction proceeds as reactants are added. Such reactors are used in a variety of applications such as colorimetric titration in the laboratory to large openhearth furnace for steel production The composition ofvolume within the reactor is not uniform; it must be made over a differential element ofvolume and integrated across the whole reactor. For a semi batch reactor all four, i.e. rate of reactant, flow out of element, rate ofreactant lost due to chemical reaction and rate of accumulation of reactant in the element must be taken into account. Semi-batch operations involves continuous addition of one ofthe reactants (A) to a tank containing the other reactant (E) initially and withdrawing the product continuously. The operation may be involved if no product is withdrawn and the volume ofthe tank is continuously changing with respect to time.
